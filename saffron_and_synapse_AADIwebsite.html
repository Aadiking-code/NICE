About Saffron And Synapse

"Empowering Abilities. Connecting Rights, Services & Opportunities in India."

— Mission of Saffron And Synapse

What does our name mean?

Saffron — Drawn from India's identity, saffron symbolises warmth, courage and vitality. It honours the richness of our diversity and the strength of every individual.

Synapse — The junction in the nervous system that connects and transmits. Here, it represents the bridge between people, policies, services and opportunities — a living connection for inclusion.

Our Vision

  • An India where every person with a disability can live with full dignity and equal opportunity
  • A society that is inclusive by design and equitable in practice
  • Digital accessibility that leaves no one behind
  • Informed communities — PwDs, caregivers, educators and policymakers — working together

Constitutional Context

The Constitution of India guarantees equality (Articles 14–16), right against discrimination, and the State's duty to make special provisions for vulnerable sections (Article 41, DPSP). India ratified the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (UNCRPD) in 2007, leading to the landmark RPwD Act 2016.

Disability Categories in India

As defined under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 — 21 specified disabilities

The RPwD Act 2016 expanded the list from 7 to 21 categories of disabilities, adopting a rights-based approach aligned with UNCRPD. Any person with at least 40% benchmark disability is entitled to rights and benefits under this Act.

Rights & Legal Framework

India's constitutional, legislative and international commitments to disability rights

  • Section 3: Right to equality, non-discrimination and equal opportunity
  • Section 16–17: Inclusive education — government educational institutions must provide free and inclusive education to children with disabilities up to age 18
  • Section 20: Government establishments must provide 4% reservation to persons with benchmark disabilities in vacancies
  • Section 24: Reasonable accommodation in workplaces — employers must make adjustments
  • Section 40–46: Accessibility standards for the built environment, transport and information
  • Section 72: Establishment of State Disability Commissioner to redress grievances
  • Schedule: Lists all 21 specified disabilities
  • Article 14: Right to Equality — no person shall be denied equality before law
  • Article 15(1): Prohibition of discrimination based on any ground
  • Article 16: Equal opportunity in public employment
  • Article 21: Right to life with dignity — interpreted to include right to live with dignity for PwDs
  • Article 41 (DPSP): State shall make effective provision for securing right to work, education and public assistance in cases of disablement
  • Article 46 (DPSP): State to promote educational and economic interests of weaker sections

India ratified the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (UNCRPD) in October 2007, committing to a rights-based approach to disability. Key UNCRPD principles reflected in Indian law:

  • Respect for inherent dignity and individual autonomy
  • Non-discrimination and full and effective participation
  • Respect for difference and acceptance of PwDs as part of human diversity
  • Equality of opportunity and accessibility
  • Equality between men and women with disabilities
  • 4% reservation in all government jobs (Group A, B, C, D)
  • 5% reservation in higher educational institutions (many states)
  • Reservation in housing allocation by government authorities
  • Age relaxation: 10 years (General), 13 years (OBC), 15 years (SC/ST) in govt. exams
  • Concessions in railway travel, airline tickets and bus services
  • Priority in passport, banking and other services
  • Tax benefits under Income Tax Act (Section 80DD, 80DDB, 80U)

Education & Employment

Rights, opportunities and support for PwDs in learning and livelihood

🏫
Inclusive Education
  • Free and compulsory education up to age 18 (RPwD Act, Sec 16)
  • Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan / SAMAGRA Shiksha — inclusive classrooms
  • Special schools under state governments
  • Extra time (25–50%) in board and competitive exams
  • Scribe/writer provision for those with arm/visual disabilities
  • Exemption from third language in many state boards
  • Home-based education for children who cannot attend school
📋
Scholarships & Fellowships
  • Pre-Matric Scholarship — Class 9–10 (DEPwD)
  • Post-Matric Scholarship — Class 11 to PhD (DEPwD)
  • Top Class Education — elite institutions (NIT, IIT, AIIMS)
  • National Fellowship for PwDs — UGC (Rs 31,000/month)
  • AICTE scholarships for technical education
  • Rajiv Gandhi National Fellowship (SC/ST PwDs)
  • State-specific scholarships — check your state portal
🛒
Employment & Reservation
  • 4% reservation in all central government jobs
  • Age relaxation: 10 yrs (General), 13 yrs (OBC), 15 yrs (SC/ST)
  • National Career Service Portal (NCSP) for disability jobs
  • Special Employment Exchanges for PwDs
  • UPSC and SSC — accessibility accommodations available
  • DDRS scheme — NGO-run vocational training programmes
  • Startup India — priority for PwD entrepreneurs
🌟
Competitive Exam Accommodations
  • UPSC — scribe, extra time, accessible centres
  • SSC — 20 extra minutes per hour for VI/OH candidates
  • NEET — compensatory time, scribe
  • JEE — accessibility facilities at exam centres
  • UGC NET — scribe and extra time
  • Bank PO/Clerk — SBI, RBI, IBPS accommodate PwDs
  • Railway exams — Grade 1–4 accommodations

Health & Rehabilitation

Government health services, assistive technology and early intervention resources

🏥
National Institutes of Disability

India has seven National Institutes under DEPwD providing specialised rehabilitation, research and training:

  • NIMH Secunderabad — Intellectual Disabilities
  • NIVH Dehradun — Visual Impairment
  • AYJNIHH Mumbai — Hearing Impairment
  • NIOH Kolkata — Locomotor Disability
  • NIEPID Secunderabad — Autism, CP, Deaf-Blind
  • NIEPVD Cuttack — Multiple Disabilities
  • SVNIRTAR Cuttack — Physical Rehabilitation
🤖
Assistive Technology & Devices
  • Screen readers: NVDA (free), JAWS, Supernova, Orca (Linux)
  • Mobility aids: Wheelchairs, crutches, prosthetics (free via ADIP)
  • Hearing: Hearing aids, cochlear implants (government subsidised)
  • Visual: Braille kits, white canes, magnifiers
  • AAC devices: Augmentative communication for non-verbal PwDs
  • Software: Dragon Naturally Speaking (voice), OpenDyslexic font
🧠
Mental Health Services
  • National Mental Health Programme (NMHP) — free services in district hospitals
  • NIMHANS Bengaluru — premier referral centre
  • Mental Health Act, 2017 — rights of persons with mental illness
  • iCall (TISS) — free counselling helpline: 9152987821
  • Vandrevala Foundation Helpline: 1860-2662-345
  • State mental hospitals — free inpatient services
👶
Early Intervention & Children
  • Early detection through Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)
  • Free screening of children 0–18 years at district hospitals
  • Cochlear implant programme for children with hearing loss
  • NPPCD — National Programme for Prevention & Control of Deafness
  • UIDAI — disability can be noted in Aadhaar for service access
  • Anganwadi centres — early childhood support including disability

Niramaya Health Insurance (National Trust)

Niramaya provides health insurance for persons with Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation and Multiple Disabilities. Coverage up to Rs 1 lakh/year. Annual premium: Rs 250 (BPL) or Rs 500 (others). Available through the National Trust's local level committee network.
